Biography

Jesus Antonio Urquijo León was born and raised in Hermosillo, Sonora, where his love for music began at age 10. At 11, he learned to play guitar on an instrument his mother bought for just $100 pesos — money he earned working construction and helping at his aunt's pizzeria.

Growing up in a family of musicians — his father, grandfathers, and uncles all played — music ran in his blood. At age 15 he began writing his own songs, and by 17 (2016) he moved to Culiacán, Sinaloa to join the group "Nuevo Giro", where he met his idol Gerardo Ortiz.

Today, Jesus Urquijo performs as a solo artist in Hermosillo with a growing catalog of original music. His breakout track "Power Trae" — featuring Hector Rubio and Los Amigos Del Requinto — has amassed over 2 million streams on Spotify. He also writes for other artists, including "Coco Psycho" for Javier Arvayo (2026).
